The future of software development is autonomous.

We're building toward a world where AI agents handle the mechanics of coding so developers can focus on what matters — designing systems, solving problems, and creating impact.

Explore our vision

We believe the future of software development is one where developers work at the speed of thought — describing what they want to build in natural language while AI agents handle implementation, testing, and deployment.

Our vision for 2030

01

Self-driving codebases

Codebases that maintain themselves. Agents automatically triage issues, write fixes, manage dependencies, and keep documentation in sync. Developers focus on architecture and product decisions.

02

AI-augmented teams

Every engineer works with a team of AI agents that specialize in different areas — frontend, backend, testing, security, and devops. Agents collaborate and review each other's work.

03

Natural language as code

Describing software in natural language becomes the primary way to build. Agents translate intent into production-quality code, tests, and documentation across any stack.

04

Continuous learning systems

Agents learn from every code review, every bug fix, and every deployment. They understand your team's conventions, preferences, and architectural decisions better than any human could.

Where we are today

Present

Assisted development

Zod agents help with coding tasks, code review, and Tab autocomplete. Developers remain in full control, using AI as a powerful assistant.

Near-term

Autonomous workflows

Agents handle end-to-end features independently — from planning to PR to deployment. Developers review and approve instead of building from scratch.

Future

Self-driving systems

Codebases that maintain and evolve themselves. Agents manage the full lifecycle of software — building, testing, deploying, monitoring, and iterating.

Why this matters

Software is eating the world, but we're not training enough developers to meet demand. AI agents can democratize software creation — enabling more people to build, and enabling professional developers to accomplish more than ever before.
